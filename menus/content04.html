This case is a guide to Case Binding:

Materials:

  • Some love (my trademark secret ingredient, getting stronger)
  • Book block (a set of pages sewn or glued together)
  • Endpapers (thick paper for the inside covers of the book)
  • Book cloth or paper (for the cover)
  • Book board (heavy cardboard for the cover)
  • Bookbinding glue
  • Ruler or any measuring tool
  • Cutting mat
  • Cutting knife
  • Bone folder (for creasing paper)

Steps:

  1. Measure the width and height of your book block, and add a few millimeters to each measurement to determine the size of your cover.
  2. Cut your book cloth or paper to the size you just calculated.
  3. Cut two pieces of book board to the same size as your cover.
  4. Apply a thin layer of bookbinding glue to each book board, and attach them to the front and back covers of your book cloth or paper.
  5. Use a bone folder to crease the book cloth or paper at the top and bottom edges of the book board to create hinges for the cover.
  6. Cut two endpapers to the same size as your book, and stick them to the inside covers of your book cloth or paper with glue, covering the book board and the hinges you just created making it look cleaner, maybe.
  7. Apply a thin layer of glue to the spine of your book block, and attach it to the spine of your cover. Use a bookbinding weight to keep the book block compressed, making it strongerer.
  8. After making sure everything is dry, apply a thin layer of glue to the inside of the book cloth or paper cover, and fold it over the edges of the book board, attaching it to the endpapers. Use a bone folder to smooth out any wrinkles or air bubbles.
  9. Put your book under a bookbinding weight to compress it, and leave it for several hours or overnight to dry completely. And done, yey!
