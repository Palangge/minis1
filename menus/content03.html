Somehow a guide to Smyth Sewn Binding:

Materials:

  • Some love (my trademark secret ingredient, it's perma now)
  • Paper (the actual pages lol)
  • Bookbinding thread
  • Bookbinding needle
  • Bookbinding glue
  • Bookbinding brush
  • Bookbinding board
  • Bookbinding cloth or paper
  • Bookbinding weight
  • Bookbinding guillotine or knife

Steps:

  1. Sew the signatures (a.k.a. folded pages) together using the Smyth Sewing method. This involves punching holes along the spine of each signature and sewing them together using bookbinding thread and a bookbinding needle.
  2. After sewing all the signatures, apply a thin layer of bookbinding glue to the spine using a bookbinding brush.
  3. Cut two pieces of bookbinding cloth or paper to the same height as the book and approximately two times the width of the spine.
  4. Apply a thin layer of bookbinding glue to the outside of the bookbinding cloth or paper and carefully wrap it around the spine.
  5. Apply pressure to the bookbinding cloth or paper to ensure it adheres to the glued spine. Use a bookbinding weight to keep the text block compressed and flat while the glue dries, Ensuring it to be strongerrer.
  6. Once the glue is dry, trim the uneven part (not the spine, don't cut the spine) of the book using a bookbinding guillotine or knife to make them even.
  7. Cut two pieces of bookbinding board to the same size as the book.
  8. Apply a thin layer of glue to the bookbinding board and carefully adhere it to the front and back of the book. Use the bookbinding weight to ensure the covers are firmly attached.
  9. Cut two pieces of bookbinding cloth or paper to the same size as the covers and wrap them around the covers.
  10. Apply pressure to the covers to ensure the cloth or paper adheres to the glued parts. Use the bookbinding weight to keep the covers compressed and flat while the glue dries.
  11. Once the glue is dry, your Smyth Sewn bound book is complete!
